> The world has been quite lively these days; ever since Trump took office, the media seems to have been constantly buzzing.

但是我想这些和普通人关系不大吧，我们需要更多关注身边的事情，做好当下的事。
> But I think these things have little to do with ordinary people. We need to pay more attention to the matters around us and focus on doing well in the present.

如果一件大事真的切身和我们产生了联系，我们实际能做的也很有限。 想象下如果我们是卢沟桥事变时北平城里的百姓；或者我们就是现在伊朗的普通百姓，或者不用想象，回想下几年前新冠疫情期间居家隔离的我们。即使在这个信息已经超级发达的时代，我们个体的判断力，抵御大变革的能力还是十分有限。
> If a major event truly affects us personally, there is actually very little we can do. Imagine if we were ordinary citizens in Beiping during the Lugou Bridge Incident; or if we are ordinary people in Iran today; or no need to imagine—recall ourselves during the home quarantine in the COVID-19 pandemic a few years ago. Even in this era of highly advanced information, our individual judgment and ability to withstand great upheavals remain very limited.

我想我们普通人最多能做的就是当这样的变革到来之后，能尽可能在最短时间里做到逃离就已经很强了。 参考抗战期间逃亡到大后方的，1952年去香港的张爱玲，上海封城期间买到飞机票的。
> I think the most ordinary people can do when such a transformation occurs is to escape as quickly as possible, which in itself is already a great feat. For example, during the War of Resistance, fleeing to the rear areas; Zhang Ailing, who went to Hong Kong in 1952; or those who managed to buy plane tickets during the Shanghai lockdown.


所以少刷新闻，至少少用看热闹看八卦的心态看新闻；多看书多学习，多锻炼多攒钱，就是我们普通人能做的事情。
> So, read the news less often, and at least avoid approaching it with a mindset of seeking gossip or sensationalism; instead, read more books, study more, exercise more, and save more money—these are the things ordinary people can do.
